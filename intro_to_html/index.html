Budgies

In The Wild

Melopsittacus undulatus, commonly known as the budgie, is a species of small parakeets native (and iconic to) to Austrailia. They live in open habitats, and usually gather in small nomadic flocks. They are mostly herbivorous, feeding on seeds and grass. They nest in hollow trees or logs and lay 4-6 eggs.

In the Pet Trade

Budgies are a common bird in the pet trade. Pet budgies have a larger average size than their wild kin. In captivity, budgies are known to mimic human speech and other sounds. They usually live 5-8 years in captivity, but longer lifespans have been recorded.

Color Morphs

Domestic Budgies have had multiple color mutations bred into their lineage. A more complete list can be seen here.

  • Green: The wild coloration, a bright green with a yellow head and blue cheek patches.
  • Blue: A light blue body with a white head. Minor marking colors remain the same.
  • Dilute: A mutation effecting several varieties, resulting in green muting to yellow and blue muting to white.
  • Cinnamon: A mutation that effects the black markings on a budgie, making them a dark brown color.
